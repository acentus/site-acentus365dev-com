Helping to prevent influenza

The single best way to prevent seasonal flu is to get vaccinated each year, but good health habits like covering your cough and washing your hands often can help stop the spread of germs and prevent respiratory illnesses like the flu. The following tips and resources below will help you learn about steps to protect yourself and others from flu. Get Vaccinated

Avoid close contact.
Avoid close contact with people who are sick. When you are sick, keep your distance from others to protect them from getting sick too.

Stay home when you are sick.
If possible, stay home from work, school, and errands when you are sick. This will help prevent spreading your illness to others.

Cover your mouth and nose.
Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue when coughing or sneezing. It may prevent those around you from getting sick. Flu and other serious respiratory illnesses, like respiratory syncytial virus (RSV), whooping cough, and severe acute respiratory syndrome (SARS), are spread by cough, sneezing, or unclean hands.

Clean your hands.
Washing your hands often will help protect you from germs. If soap and water are not available, use an alcohol-based hand rub.

Avoid touching your eyes, nose or mouth.
Germs are often spread when a person touches something that is contaminated with germs and then touches his or her eyes, nose, or mouth.

Practice other good health habits.
Clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces at home, work or school, especially when someone is ill. Get plenty of sleep, be physically active, manage your stress, drink plenty of fluids, and eat nutritious food.

Preventing Falls

An older adult is treated for an injury resulting from a fall every 13 seconds. Common reasons for these falls are tripping over loose rugs, reduced muscle strength and medication side effects. Falls can be prevented by the use of a cane or walker, keeping rooms clutter-free, using skid-proof backs on rugs and adding additional lighting for better navigation. The National Council On Aging has developed resources to help reduce the risk of falls. Please click here to review the NCOA’s website that contains helpful tips, videos and programs on fall prevention.

Emergency Management

Florida is prone to natural disasters such as tropical storms, hurricanes and tornadoes. People living with medical conditions may require extra assistance to maintain health during one of these emergency events. FloridaDisaster.org is a state resource designed to direct you to your specific county’s medical special needs resource, including registering with a special needs shelter for those requiring a higher level of care.
